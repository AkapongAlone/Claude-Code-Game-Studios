Every approved art bible section (S1–S6) follows these conventions without exception:

**Voice:** Analytical, declarative, no hedging. Rules are stated as rules, not suggestions. "Must" and "never" are used precisely and mean what they say.

**Structure per section:** Opening rationale → reference to relevant design pillar → table-based specifications where multiple items exist → design tests at section end.

**Design tests:** Every section closes with one or more "> **Design test**:" callouts. These are concrete, testable scenarios — not abstract quality checks. A design test describes a specific action someone can take to verify the principle holds ("line up all seven portraits and cover names").

**Flags:** Unresolved production decisions or cross-domain dependencies are labeled "Flag [Letter]:" with the letter carrying over across sections. These are explicit placeholders, not ambiguity.

**No orphaned principles:** Every principle references a design pillar (P1–P4) via italic anchor text. If a principle cannot be anchored to a pillar, it is questioned.

**Tables for anything with 3+ items:** Color swatches, officer differentiators, semantic colors, terrain types, prop densities — all tabular. Prose for philosophy; tables for specifications.

**Why:** The user approved sections through S6 with this voice and structure intact. Maintaining it ensures the bible reads as one authored document, not a patchwork.

**How to apply:** Before drafting any new art bible section, re-read one approved section to recalibrate the register. Do not introduce hedging language ("may want to consider"), do not drop design tests, do not orphan a principle from its pillar.
